The Miss Miami Gardens Scholarship Program is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit that seeks to provide scholarship assistance to young women in Miami Gardens, FL. Our organization continues to provide women a dynamic platform to effectuate positive change in their communities through service and scholarship. Title holders are provided a year-round opportunity to earn academic assistance, grow their professional networks, learn empowering life skills, and be the change they want to see in the world. Join us as we continue to prepare great women for the world and prepare the world for great women.

ABOUT THE MISS MIAMI GARDENS

OUTSTANDING TEEN SCHOLARSHIP COMPETITION










Candidates in the Miss Miami Gardens Outstanding TEEN Competition gain experience through participation in interviews,

talent performances, public speaking, promoting social impact initiatives, and social media interaction.

Cash and in-kind educational scholarships are available to all young women who advance to the Miss Florida state competition.

  1. 1. Private Interview
  2. 2. On Stage Conversation
  3. 3. Health & Fitness
  4. 4. Performance Talent/HERStory
  5. 5. Evening Gown







Miss Miami Gardens Outstanding TEEN Competition Scoring



  1. 30% Private Interview
  2. 10% On Stage Conversation
  3. 20% Health & Fitness
  4. 20% Performance Talent/HERStory
  5. 20% Evening Gown







    6. Social Impact:



    1. Each candidate will be required to have a Social Impact Initiative which they will advocate during their year of service as Miss Miami Gardens. Each candidate should select a Social Impact Initiative in relation to a cause or issue that she has a strong personal commitment, involvement, or conviction.


    2. A candidate’s Social Impact Initiative allows her to explain to the judges and members of the community a subject matter of her choice, how she chooses to advocate for it during her year and the metrics she plans to use for success at the close of her year of service. In addition, it should explain how her advocacy will further the mission of the Miss Florida’s Outstanding TEEN & Miss America’s Outstanding TEEN Organizations.







      3. Miss America’s Outstanding TEEN Scoring System



        1. INTERVIEW



        1. The Private Interview competition is the first opportunity to meet the panel of judges. The duration of the interview will be 8 minutes.  During this time each contestant will be able to converse with the judges about their goals and aspirations as well as express their personal opinions. The judges will be looking for a sharp and tasteful young woman who is sure of herself, current events and her Social Impact Initiative. This portion of the competition counts for 30% of the final score.







          2. 2. LIFESTYLE & WELLNESS/SPORTSWEAR



          1. The Lifestyle & Wellness phase of the competition is designed to see how well the candidate maintains a lifestyle of good physical health and whether she has the sense of confidence and self-assurance needed to be a titleholder. This phase is performed in active / sportswear, not swimsuits. This portion of the competition counts for 10% of the final score.







            2. 3. TALENT



            1. The Talent phase of competition allows each candidate to demonstrate artistic craft.  The presentation will not be more than 90 seconds in duration.  Some examples of talents performed within the Miss America Scholarship Competition include singing, dancing, playing a musical instrument, comic or dramatic reading, baton, acrobatic movements, etc.  Each candidate is responsible for providing their own music or arrangements. 


            2. Judges will be looking for interpretive ability, technical skill level, stage presence and integration of elements (costume, choreography, music, and props) used in the presentation. Talent represents 40% of the final score. 







              3. 4. EVENING WEAR & ON-STAGE QUESTION



              1. The Red Carpet / Evening Wear & On-Stage Question phase of competition is 20% of the total score.   


              2. The judges are looking for: overall first impression, sense of confidence, walk, posture, carriage and grace, personality, stage presence, beauty and sense of attractiveness, appropriateness of the evening wear. 


              3. A note on wardrobe selection in all phases of competition: Candidates should always choose something that compliments her personality. Candidates are judged on the total look and overall first impression; not the outfit itself or price of the outfit. Each candidate should choose a style, color, and fit, that is becoming to her. 


              4. Immediately following the candidate’s modeling of her evening wear, she will answer an On-stage Question. The question is essentially an extension of the private interview. The time limit to answer the question is 20 seconds and a timer will be used.


              5. The judges will be looking for personality, intelligence, speech, vocabulary, and style. 







                6. CANDIDATE AGE & RESIDENCY REQUIREMENTS



                1. Miss Miami Gardens candidates must be between 19-28 years of age.


                2. Miss Miami Gardens’ Outstanding TEEN candidates must be between 13- 18 years of age.


                3. Candidates must reside, work, or be successfully enrolled in and physically attending classes on a full-time basis at an accredited college or technical school in the City of Miami Gardens, FL or Opa-Locka, FL, reside, work, or attend school in one of the following approved address zip codes: 33013, 33014, 33054, 33055, 33056, 33169, 33175, 33179, 33269.







                ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS



                1. Candidates must be in reasonably good health to meet the job requirements.


                2. Participate in fundraising for the Miss America Foundation.


                3. Meet character criteria set forth by the Miss America Organization.


                4. Candidates must be able to meet the time commitment and job responsibilities as set forth by the Miss Miami Gardens Scholarship Program, Inc. should she win the title. (The titleholder will enter a contract with the organization)


                5. Candidates must not now, nor have ever been married, nor has had a marriage annulled, nor will get married during the year of service to the organization.







                  6. APPLICATION



                  1. First, you will need to register through the Miss America website. You will need to select the correct state (FLORIDA) and enter “Miss Miami Gardens” as the competition you would like to enter.


                  2. Second, enter your information there to receive the application packet.
